# CSMS Certificate Lifecycle Management
Firetruck Security Platform  
UNECE R155 – CSMS Certificate (§6.3–§6.11)

## 1. Initial CSMS Assessment and Certification

1. CSMS documentation and evidence are compiled into a CSMS Certificate Package:
   - CSMS_Declaration.md
   - CSMS_Scope.md
   - CSMS_Processes.md
   - CSMS_Traceability_Matrix.md
   - Risk and threat registers
   - Logs and snapshots
   - Annual R155 report

2. The package is submitted to the Technical Service / Approval Authority for assessment.

3. Upon successful assessment, a CSMS certificate is issued:
   - Validity: up to 3 years (UNECE R155 §6.7)
   - Scope: Firetruck Security Platform and associated vehicle types.

## 2. Ongoing CSMS Operation

During the certificate validity period, the manufacturer:

- Maintains CSMS processes as documented.
- Keeps risk assessments and mitigations up to date.
- Monitors threats, vulnerabilities, and incidents.
- Generates annual R155 reports.
- Maintains logs, snapshots, and evidence.

## 3. Change Management and Notification (§6.9)

The manufacturer identifies and documents CSMS-relevant changes, including:

- Process changes
- Organizational changes
- Tooling changes
- New or modified vehicle types
- New or modified suppliers
- New or modified update mechanisms (linked to SUMS)

For significant changes, the Approval Authority is notified in line with §6.9, using:

- A CSMS Change Summary
- Updated CSMS documentation
- Updated traceability and evidence where applicable

## 4. Certificate Renewal (§6.10)

Before certificate expiry:

1. Review and update:
   - CSMS documentation
   - Traceability matrix
   - Risk and threat registers
   - Supplier risk documentation
   - Annual R155 reports
   - Logs and snapshots

2. Compile a CSMS Renewal Package:
   - Updated CSMS_Declaration.md
   - Updated CSMS_Evidence_Index.md
   - Summary of changes since last assessment
   - Latest annual R155 report
   - Key evidence samples

3. Submit the renewal package to the Approval Authority for reassessment.

## 5. Expiry or Withdrawal (§6.11)

If the CSMS certificate:

- Expires without renewal, or  
- Is withdrawn by the Approval Authority,

then:

- Vehicle type approvals relying on this CSMS may be affected.
- New approvals or significant modifications may be blocked until a valid CSMS is re-established.
- The manufacturer must restore compliance and seek re-certification.

## 6. Records and Retention

The manufacturer retains:

- CSMS certificates and related correspondence
- CSMS documentation and evidence
- Annual R155 reports
- Change notifications and renewal packages

for at least the duration of vehicle type approvals and any additional period required by applicable regulations.
